### 3. `analytics/pull_trends.py` — rate-limit structural fix

**File changed:** `analytics/pull_trends.py`

- **Rotating UA:** 5 user-agent strings (macOS/Win/Linux, Safari/Chrome/Firefox mix); one is picked per client.
- **Fresh client per group:** instead of reusing one `TrendReq`, build a new one for `core_product`, `job_search`, `competitors`, `intent_signals`, `emerging`, and the geo pull. Google rate-limits at the session level — breaking the chain of calls across sessions reduces trigger likelihood.
- **Exponential backoff with jitter on 429:** 8s → 16s → 32s → 64s → 128s, plus 0-50% jitter. Up to 5 retries per call. Detects 429, "too many", "timed out", "connection" error variants as transient.
- **Inter-group sleep:** random 12-20s between groups so Google sees spaced traffic, not a burst.
- **Related-queries best-effort:** if `related_queries()` 429s, log and move on rather than cascading the failure to the next group.
